College Acceptance

We know how hard it is to be admitted to the college of your choice. The scores you achieve on your SAT/ACT can have a large bearing on whether you are accepted at your favorite schools. A recent study by the National Association For College Admission Counseling found "a small increase in test score could have a significant impact on an applicant’s chances of being admitted." Furthermore, the Wall Street Journal has found that your scores can have an impact well after college because employers are now using SAT scores as an employment qualification tool. We had great success last summer helping students achieve the scores they will need to attend their college of choice.

Incredible Results

The initial session of our innovative program took place the summer of 2015 and generated unbelievable results for the 5 sophomores participating:

  • 334 Point Average Score Improvement:
    By improving an average of 334 points on the 2400 point SAT, the 5 sophomore students dramatically out performed students in traditional tutoring programs. In the article “SAT Coaching Found to Boost Scores — Barely”, The Wall Street Journal reported that “SAT coaching resulted in about 30 points in score improvement on the SAT, out of a possible 1600”.

  • All 5 Students Scored in the Top 4% Worldwide:
    Each earned at least a 2100 combined score. A 2100 is viewed to be the baseline score needed to get into most top colleges and universities.

  • 3 Student Scored in the Top 0.5% Worldwide for ACT:
    Even though we didn’t focus on the ACT last year, three students scored in the top 0.5% worldwide. They accomplished this by earning a 34 or greater composite score which is better than 99.5% of all 1.8 million ACT test takers.

  • 1 Perfect Score:
    1.7 million students took the test in 2015, only 583 achieved a perfect score of 2400 - it’s incredibly hard. InspireDo helped one of its students achieve this rare feat.

We increased the program to 15 students this past summer (2016), and had similar success:

  • 5 Point Average ACT Score Improvement:
    By improving an average of 5 points on the Composite Score of the ACT, the 15 sophomore students dramatically out performed students in traditional tutoring programs. In the article “SAT Coaching Found to Boost Scores — Barely”, The Wall Street Journal reported that coaching “resulted in less than one point out of a possible 36 on the ACT.”

  • 9 Students Scored in the Top 7% Worldwide:
    Each earned at least a 30 combined score. A 30 is viewed to be the baseline score needed to get into most top colleges and universities.

  • 2 Perfect Scores:
    Nearly 2.1 million students took the test in 2016, only 1,598 achieved a perfect score of 36 - it’s incredibly hard. InspireDo helped two of its students achieve this rare feat.
