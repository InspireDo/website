Pricing

The cost of the InspireDo program is $2,500 and is due before taking the baseline exam. The program is designed for rising juniors. The three phases of the program are:

  • Intensive work: June 24 (tentative) to August 23, one hour of practice per day for six days per week
  • Test time: August 24 to October 5, one to two hours of practice per week
  • Continued support (optional): October 7 to fall of senior year, continued support with materials/tutoring, practice is self-scheduled

The InspireDo program fee includes:

  • A unique mind training and breathing app designed by Inspire Do (for use on an iPhone)
  • Practice tests which are official-content, full-length, and previously-used ACT and SAT tests
  • Four proctored, full-length practice tests administered in June, July and August on paper
  • Nine weeks of tutoring (2x week, 30-60 minutes per session)
  • Daily monitoring of activity and prompt response to questions and concerns
  • Weekly progress updates for parents
  • If not delighted with the program you may request a refund up to $1,550 by November 1, 2019*

    • * All support is discontinued once a refund is requested

InspireDo vs. Traditional Tutoring

To provide a frame of reference on the effectiveness of traditional tutoring, the Wall Street Journal reported the following:

The college counselors' report concludes that, on average, prep courses yield only a modest benefit, "contrary to the claims made by many test-preparation providers." It found that SAT coaching resulted in about 30 points in score improvement on the SAT, out of a possible 1600, and less than one point out of a possible 36 on the ACT, the other main college-entrance exam.

—WSJ Article: “SAT Coaching Found to Boost Scores—Barely”
